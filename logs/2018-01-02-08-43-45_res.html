Lefty's Food Club Bets

Welcome to Lefty's Food Club Bets! I've been playing Food Club for seven years and it's my favorite game on Neopets. I am a regular in the regular competition hosted on the FC chat thread in the Games forum.

The simplest way to describe Food Club is that it is like betting on a horse race, except you're betting on which pirates will win the daily eating contest. It is a gambling game and there is some risk involved. I strongly advise that you not bet more than you are prepared to lose. You will win some days, and you will lose some days. With some luck and good bets you will see a nice profit over time.

For players just starting out and want to make their own bets, I recommend reading Revenge's Guide and Sir Hatter's Guide for more information about the game and suggested betting strategies. Links to both of them are in the sidebar.

My Betting Style

Strictly speaking, I don't follow a system; however, I do have guidelines which I follow.

1. As a general rule, I like to take some risk because it is: (a) more fun and interesting to do so in my opinion; and (b) more profitable over time. However, I don't get too crazy, and most of the time I incorporate safeties in my betting.

2. My favorite type of card is when there is at least one arena with a clearly favored pirate (meaning there is a 2:1 pirate and the other pirates are 5:1 or greater). It's better still when at least two of the pirates are long shots (at 10:1 to 13:1). The more arenas like this on a given day the better.

3. Whenever possible I try to incorporate bets on pirates that have a positive Estimated Value (EV).

4. I avoid making bets that will yield less then 10:1 (breaking even), unless there's a good chance of a cascade. Then they add up, which is good.

5. While I do pay attention to Food Adjustments, I believe that the opening odds are the best indicators of outcome.

Food Club in a Nutshell

20 hungry pirates randomly assigned to 5 arenas for each match. One pirate from each arena will win in each match. For a bet to win, all of the pirates contained in that bet must win!

Odds of winning go from 2:1 (the best, most favorable) up to 13:1 (long shot, least favorable).

Any pirate in each arena has a chance to win.

The maximum amount that you can bet each day is based on your account age. It is 50 + 2 for every day you have been playing Neopets.

The more you bet the more you can win. If you can afford it, why not bet the max amount? You don't have to, of course.

There is a limit of 10 bets per match. The maximum amount that you can win from an individual bet is 1 million np.

FC Notes

1. If you choose to copy another player's bets then you really should make all 10 bets. They are designed to work together to ensure coverage of different possible outcomes. The idea is to maximize your chance of winning, however there are no guarantees.

2. Keep in mind that this is a gambling game and unexpected outcomes can and do happen. Don't bet more neopoints than you are prepared to lose. Follow these bets (or any other player's for that matter) at your own risk.

3. If you want to try for the trophy then you can hold a maximum of 8 consecutive days of winnings (bust days count). If you are holding after the round on the eighth day then you MUST collect before midnight NST or you will forfeit the winnings from the first day of your hold. Assuming you are aiming for a trophy at the beginning of the month, a good way to remember when to start holding is to go by the day of the week of the first of the month. For example, if the first of the month is going to fall on a Monday, then start your trophy run on the preceding Monday (or Tuesday/Tuesday, and so on).

4. Please note that the odds can change throughout the day. The odds shown in my posted bets reflect what they were at the time that I updated the page.
